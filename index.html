Hello, my name is Kangsan!

I am an undergraduate student in Industrial Management Engineering at Korea University. I am interested in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and User Experience (UX).

Projects

Scholar's Success Drive: Donation matching service

Project for Human Centered Computing (CS-00, Fall 2024)

This project focuses on enhancing interactions with iOS devices using the latest Nearby Interaction framework. It allows users to engage with devices in unique and creative ways.

Role: UI/UX Design, Prototyping, Experimental Design

Motion Sickness Related Factors in Autonomous Driving Situation

Project for Experimental Design and Statistical Analysis (IMEN339-00, Spring 2024)

This study analyzes the effects of driving skill, driving speed, and vehicle height on motion sickness levels. A 3×3×2 factorial experimental design was applied to design an experiment using an autonomous driving simulator, and Three-Way ANOVA was conducted to verify main and interaction effects.

Role: Ideation, Experimental Design, Statistical Analysis

KLIB2: Improving UI and User Experience in Library Reservation Systems

Project for Ergonomics (IMEN315-00, Spring 2024)

A UI improvement plan for the KLIB2 library reservation system at Korea University was designed and tested with 30 participants, demonstrating reduced task completion time and increased user satisfaction compared to the existing UI. Based on the findings, the proposed improvements were presented to enhance implementation feasibility, with further research considering enhancements to the quick reservation feature.

Role: Ideation, Prototyping, Experimental Design

Defining UX Factors and Prioritizing Improvements for Chatbot Services: A Personalization Perspective on Interaction

Project for UX and AI (IMEN319-00, Spring 2024)

To address the issue of chatbots lacking long-term memory and failing to retain conversation context, this study proposes a context management approach using conversation history storage and logical operations. This enables the development of personalized chatbots, enhancing user experience and satisfaction.

Role: Ideation

SPAT: Enhancing Online Course Completion through Study Partner Matching

Project for Service Planning Bootcamp (Winter 2024)

SPAT is a study partner matching service that helps increase online course completion rates by forming study groups, providing reminders, and offering refunds based on participation. Features like voice meetings and Q&A sessions further enhance learning efficiency.

Role: Ideation, Information Architecture
